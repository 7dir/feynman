scale(x)¶

Apply a scaling factor to the diagram. Suppose your diagram looks good for a figure of size (6,6) with a single subplot. If you create multiple subplots in your figure, each with its own diagram (e.g. to represent an equation), then the same diagram will generally look too thick in the smaller subplots. It is then desirable to apply a scaling factor to thin down the lines, the arrows, and the text for all objects of the diagram.
